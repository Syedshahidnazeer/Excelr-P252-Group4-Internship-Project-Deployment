DATA VISUALIZATIONS

Word Cloud


Our initial dataset contains 10,607,577 words across all of the news articles combined. After pre-processing our data by removing stop words and lemmatization, our final dataset consisted of 214,805 unique words. The following word cloud was a visual depiction of all distinct words trained into our model, varying in size depending on the frequency of news articles.

Stacked Bar


We were interested in looking at the most repeated words in our dataset, The most frequency words were mainly related to U.S. presidents, most notably Donald Trump. Interestingly, news articles related to Obama and Clinton had a higher distribution of fake news compared to Donald Trump.

Bar Chart


Our dataset has three main topics - Politics, World News, and U.S. News. A majority of our training data consisted of news topics related to politics. Understanding this, the prediction of our model being accurate on non-political news is not great.

Confusion Matrix


Logistic Regression is used to train our machine learning model. The training data yielded an accuracy score of 99.5%. We ended up with an accuracy score of 99.1%.
